Khinkali

Khinkali are large, soup-filled dumplings from Georgian cuisine,

made with a twisted knob of dough filled with spiced meat, cheese, or mushrooms. They are traditionally eaten by hand by first holding the "handle" at the top,

taking a bite to slurp out the broth, and then eating the rest, leaving the handle behind

Ingredients

For the dough

  • All purpose flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Egg (Optional)

For the filling

  • Ground beef and pork, 50/50 mix.
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Cumin
  • Fresh Cilantro
  • Water or Broth

Optional Regional Varieties

  • Lamb filling
  • Mushroom filling
  • Cheese filling

Step-by-Step Guide on how to make Khinkali

Make the dough

  1. Mix 3 cups flour + 1 cup water + 1 tsp salt
  2. Knead until smooth (about 8-10 min)
  3. Cover and rest 30 minutes.

Prepare the filling

  1. Mix together:
  2. 300g beef + 300 g ground pork
  3. 1 finely chopped onion
  4. 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp cumin
  5. 2-4 tbsp chopped cilantro (optinal)
  6. 1/2-1 cup cold water or broth
  7. Stir it until it becomes loose and slightly soupy

Shape the Khinkali

  1. Roll the dough think and cut circles (10-12cm)
  2. Add 1-2 tbsp filling in the center.
  3. Fold pleats around the top and pinch firmly to seal.

Boil

  1. Boil salted water
  2. Add Khinkali and cook 7-10 minutes, until they float and puff up

Serve

  1. Serve hot with freshly ground black pepper
  2. Hold by the "knob" and eat carefully --- don't spill the broth!
