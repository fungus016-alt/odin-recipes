Satsivi

Satsivi is a classic Georgian dish made of poultry (usually chicken or turkey)

served in a rich, creamy walnut-garlic sauce. The word "satsivi" actually refers to the

cold walnut sauce, not the meat itself.

Key Features

  • Served cold (traditionally during winter holidays)
  • Sauce is thick, silky, and intensely aromatic
  • Flavored with traditional Georgian spices

Basic Ingredients

  • Ground walnuts
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Georgian spices (usually khmeli-suneli, blue fenugreek, coriander)
  • Vinegar or wine vinegar
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Sometimes a bit of broth to thin the sauce
  • Poultry (chicken or turkey), cooked separately and then cooled in the sauce

Step by Step Guide

Cook the chicken

  1. Boil the chicken in salted water until fully cooked
  2. Remove chicken let it cool and cut into serving pieces
  3. Save the broth, you'll need it for the sauce

Prepare the Walnut Paste

  1. Grind walnuts very finely (almost like a paste)
  2. Add garlic and grind together again
  3. Mix in:
    • khmeli-suneli
    • blue fenugreek
    • ground coriander
    • turmeric
    • salt & Pepper

Make the Sauce

  1. Finely chop and saute the onion until soft and translucent
  2. Add the walnut paste to the pan and stir gently
  3. Slowly add warm chicken broth, little by little, until the sauce becomes thick but pourable
  4. Add the vinegar to taste

COmbine & Simmer

  1. Add the cooked chicken pieces into the walnut sauce.
  2. Simmer on very low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring gently so it doesn't burn
  3. Adjust salt, pepper, and vinegar

Cool completely

Satsivi is server cold, so let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for several hours or overnight

Serve

  • Serve chilled with bread, mchadi (cornbread), or boiled rice
  • The sauce should be thick, creamy, and aromatic
